Gerard Lynn

Agents are starting to act on our behalf. The unsolved problem isn't whether they can — it's whether we can trust them to, and trust is a structure, not a feeling: identity, permissions, consent, legibility. That structure is what I build.

At Penguin Random House since 2012, I built the company's first production LLM platform on Azure OpenAI and architected unified identity across B2B and consumer applications on Ping/DaVinci. The same problem now applies to AI agents, which act for people and therefore have to prove it: whose behalf, within what bounds, auditable afterward.

I came to the work through interaction design at NYU's ITP.
